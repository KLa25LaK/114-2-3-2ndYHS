第 35–38 題｜駭客的三種類型

📄 學生卷完整文章

The modern world relies heavily on the internet. This vast digital network is often referred to as the information superhighway. While this virtual highway allows users to communicate across the globe instantly, it is also filled with hidden dangers. Among the most discussed figures in this online space are hackers. Although movies often depict them as mysterious criminals typing rapidly in dark rooms, the reality is much more complex. A hacker is essentially a person who uses advanced computer skills to overcome digital security barriers. However, not all hackers are villains. Their intentions divide them into three distinct groups, which are commonly identified by the “colors” of imaginary hats. The most dangerous group is the black hat hackers. These are the cybercriminals that most people fear. They break into computer systems to steal sensitive data, such as credit card numbers or passwords. Their motive usually involves financial gain or a malicious desire to destroy a system. For this reason, they are the primary reason businesses invest millions in cybersecurity. On the opposite side are the white hat hackers, also known as ethical hackers. These experts use the exact same tools and skills as black hat hackers, but they do so to protect rather than to hurt. Large technology companies and banks hire them to deliberately “attack” their own networks. By acting like their nemesis, white hat hackers can find unexpected weaknesses and fix them before the real bad guys have a chance to enter. Between these two extremes are gray hat hackers. These individuals do not work for the company they are hacking, and they usually do not have permission for their actions. Unlike black hats, they generally do not want to steal money or cause damage. They might break into a network just to prove they can or to point out a flaw to the owner. Although their intent might not be evil, their actions can still be illegal, making them unpredictable figures in the digital world.

In finance, there is a unique approach to investing called the barbell strategy. It advises investors to stay away from the “middle ground,” a dangerous zone where investments carry a moderate amount of risk but offer only average returns. Instead, the strategy suggests balancing two extremes to achieve the best possible results. A person following the barbell strategy places the majority of their money, up to 90 percent, into extremely safe choices, such as cash or government bonds. This ensures that they will never lose their life savings, keeping their financial foundation secure. The remaining portion is then used for high-risk investments that have a small chance of exploding in value. If these risky bets fail, the loss is limited. However, if they succeed, the rewards can change the person’s life. Interestingly, this theory is also being used to describe a growing trend in the modern workplace: the barbell career. In the past, people sought a full-time position with one company for life. However, modern experts argue that a typical 9-to-5 office job isn’t as safe as it once might have been. In a changing economy, such roles carry the hidden risk of sudden layoffs and often limit personal freedom. To solve this problem, a barbell career combines a very stable, low-stress job with a high-risk creative passion. One end of the barbell is a “day job” that generates a steady income but might lead to mental stagnation after hours of work. Examples include working as a government clerk or a driver. This type of job covers living expenses like rent and food. The other end is a high-risk, potentially high-reward pursuit, such as writing a novel, developing an app, or creating art. By adopting this “slash” identity, like being a driver/writer or clerk/inventor, workers can enjoy financial security while still taking chances to realize their dreams. This smart balance of stability and ambition is fast becoming the mainstream choice for work for many people.

第 43–46 題｜熱帶豬籠草

📄 學生卷完整文章

Many people don’t know about the existence of meat-eating plants. In fact, they are more likely to associate such things with science fiction. However, these remarkable plants are very real. Scientifically classified under the genus Nepenthes, they are more commonly known as tropical pitcher plants. They are mainly found in Southeast Asia, though some species also grow in regions ranging from Madagascar to Australia. Their unusual appearance and survival strategy make them one of nature’s most fascinating examples of adaptation. The overall structure of a tropical pitcher plant resembles a hollow pitcher that extends from the tip of a long leaf. Positioned above the opening is a protective lid that prevents rain from entering the trap. These plants are able to survive in nutrient-poor environments, such as sandy soils and volcanic regions. They obtain essential minerals from captured prey through their digestive glands. Insects and small animals like birds and rats that fall into the plant’s large “pitcher” are often attracted by its nectar, smell, or vibrant red and green colors. Another reason is that the rim of the plant, known as the lip, becomes extremely slippery in wet weather. Once an animal falls inside, liquid surrounds the prey, and escape is almost impossible due to the waxy inner walls of the plant. Although the tropical pitcher plant is a predator, it is also prey to humans. In the Malaysian part of Borneo, locals use the plant in a dish called lemang periuk kera. The cup-like part of the plant is filled with rice, mixed with coconut milk, and steamed for an hour. The resulting treat has proven so tasty that the tropical pitcher plant is in danger of extinction due to overharvesting. Bushfires during the dry season are another factor in its population decline. Hopefully, commercial cultivation and conservation efforts may help meet human demand and offset the damage caused by natural events.
